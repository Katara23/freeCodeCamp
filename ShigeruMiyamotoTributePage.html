Shigeru Miyamoto
Video game icon

About

Shigeru Miyamoto is a video game designer, director, and producer for Nintendo. He was born on November 16, 1952 in rural Sonobe near Kyoto, Japan. His childhood explorations of the countryside became the inspiration for the exploration in The Legend of Zelda. He began his career at Nintendo in 1977 after graduating from Kanazawa College of Art. He got an interview at Nintendo because his father was a friend of Hiroshi Yamauchi, the president of the company at the time. Miyamoto is the creator of some of the most successful video game franchises in history, including Mario, The Legend of Zelda, Donkey Kong, Star Fox, F-Zero, and Pikmin. He's such a valuable employee that Nintendo won't allow him to walk or bike to work anymore!
